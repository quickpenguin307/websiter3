DIY Volcano Eruption Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this DIY volcano eruption experiment, you'll create a simulated volcanic eruption using common household materials. This experiment demonstrates an acid-base reaction and provides insights into chemical reactions and geological phenomena.

Materials Required

  • Baking soda
  • Vinegar
  • Food coloring
  • Dish soap
  • Clay or play dough (to shape the volcano)
  • A tray or container to contain the eruption
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Safety goggles (optional, but recommended)

Setting Up the Experiment

Begin by shaping the clay or play dough around a small container or bottle to form your volcano. Make sure the container's opening is at the top, as it will hold the baking soda and vinegar.

Procedure

  1. Add 2-3 tablespoons of baking soda into the container at the center of your volcano.
  2. Add a few drops of food coloring and a squirt of dish soap to enhance the visual effect.
  3. When you're ready for the eruption, slowly pour vinegar into the container and observe the reaction as it creates a bubbly, flowing eruption.

Observations

As the vinegar reacts with the baking soda, carbon dioxide gas is released, causing a frothy eruption that simulates a volcanic explosion. Notice the vigorous bubbling and the way the mixture flows over the sides of your volcano.
