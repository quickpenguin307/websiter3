Invisible Ink Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this experiment, you'll create secret messages using invisible ink. The ink will become visible when exposed to heat, making this a fun and simple science project that explores acid-base reactions and oxidation.

Materials Required

  • Lemon juice (or milk)
  • Cotton swab or paintbrush
  • White paper
  • Heat source (lamp, iron, or candle with adult supervision)

Procedure

  1. Pour a small amount of lemon juice into a bowl.
  2. Dip a cotton swab or paintbrush into the lemon juice.
  3. Use the swab to write a secret message on the white paper.
  4. Let the paper dry completely. The writing should become nearly invisible.
  5. To reveal the message, gently heat the paper by holding it near a lamp, iron, or candle (with adult supervision).

Observations

As the paper is heated, the invisible ink will darken and become visible. This happens because the acid in the lemon juice weakens the paper, and when heated, it oxidizes, turning brown.
