DIY Sundial Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

A sundial is a simple yet fascinating way to tell time using the position of the sun. In this DIY project, you’ll create your own sundial and see how the angle of the sun’s rays changes throughout the day.

Materials Required

  • 1 paper plate (or cardboard)
  • 1 pencil (or a small stick)
  • Scissors
  • Ruler
  • Markers or a pen
  • Compass (optional, for better accuracy)

Procedure

  1. Place the paper plate flat on a surface and draw a dot in the center.
  2. Cut the pencil or stick to a length of about 4 inches. This will be the gnomon (the part that casts the shadow).
  3. Attach the pencil to the center of the plate using glue or tape, making sure it stands upright (at a 90-degree angle to the plate).
  4. Mark the four main compass directions (north, south, east, west) around the edge of the plate. If you have a compass, use it to accurately mark the directions.
  5. On a sunny day, place your sundial outside in direct sunlight, making sure the gnomon points towards true north (or south in the southern hemisphere).
  6. Observe how the shadow moves throughout the day, marking each hour with a marker as the sun moves across the sky.

Observations

As the Earth rotates, the position of the sun changes, and the shadow of the gnomon moves accordingly. The sundial uses this movement to tell time based on the position of the sun in the sky.
