Static Electricity Butterfly Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

This fun experiment shows how static electricity can make a paper butterfly "fly"! By rubbing a balloon on your hair or a piece of fabric, you create static electricity that can lift and move a paper butterfly.

Materials Required

  • 1 small piece of paper (cut into the shape of a butterfly)
  • 1 balloon
  • 1 piece of fabric (such as a wool sweater or scarf)
  • 1 table or flat surface

Procedure

  1. Cut a piece of paper into the shape of a butterfly. Place it on a flat surface.
  2. Rub the balloon vigorously on your hair or a piece of fabric to build up static electricity.
  3. Hold the balloon near the paper butterfly and watch as it lifts off the surface, seemingly flying!

Observations

Rubbing the balloon on your hair or fabric transfers electrons to the balloon, giving it a negative charge. When you bring the balloon near the paper butterfly, the opposite charge in the butterfly is attracted, causing it to lift and move.
