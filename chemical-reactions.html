Overview

In this experiment, you'll explore the fascinating world of chemical reactions by observing the reaction between vinegar and baking soda. This classic demonstration shows how an acid and a base react to produce carbon dioxide gas, water, and sodium acetate.

Components Required

  • Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate)
  • White Vinegar (Acetic Acid)
  • A clear plastic bottle or glass container
  • Measuring spoons
  • Balloon (optional, to capture the gas)
  • Paper and pencil for recording observations

Chemical Reaction Explained

When vinegar (an acid) is mixed with baking soda (a base), a chemical reaction occurs that produces carbon dioxide gas, water, and sodium acetate. The rapid production of carbon dioxide gas causes bubbling and can inflate a balloon placed over the container's opening.

Procedure

  1. Place 1â€“2 teaspoons of baking soda into the clear container.
  2. If you wish to capture the gas, carefully stretch a balloon over the opening of the container.
  3. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of white vinegar into the container.
  4. Observe the reaction: bubbles will form as carbon dioxide gas is released. If using a balloon, watch it inflate.
  5. Record your observations and discuss the reaction and the gases produced.

Safety and Cleanup

This experiment uses common household materials and is safe when performed as directed. Work in a well-ventilated area and clean up any spills promptly.
