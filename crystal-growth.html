Crystal Growth Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this experiment, you will observe how crystals form from a saturated solution. Using common household materials, you will create conditions that allow crystals to grow slowly over time.

Components Required

  • Table salt or sugar
  • Water
  • A clear glass jar or container
  • Spoon for stirring
  • String or a thin stick
  • A small weight (e.g., a paperclip) to anchor the string
  • Optional: Food coloring for tinted crystals

Procedure

  1. Heat about 1 cup of water until it is nearly boiling (adult supervision is required).
  2. Slowly stir in salt (or sugar) until no more will dissolve, creating a saturated solution.
  3. If desired, add a few drops of food coloring to the solution.
  4. Pour the solution into the glass jar.
  5. Tie a piece of string to a pencil or stick so that it can rest on the rim of the jar with the string dangling into the solution. Attach a small weight to the bottom of the string to keep it straight.
  6. Place the jar in a safe, undisturbed area at room temperature. Over several days, crystals will begin to form along the string and on the jarâ€™s sides.
  7. Observe and record the crystal growth each day.

Observations

As water evaporates, the solution becomes supersaturated, and crystals start to form. The size and shape of the crystals will vary depending on the material used and environmental conditions.
