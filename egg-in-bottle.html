Egg in a Bottle Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

This classic science experiment demonstrates the effects of air pressure. By creating a pressure difference inside and outside a bottle, you can make a hard-boiled egg magically slip into the bottle without breaking it!

Materials Required

  • 1 hard-boiled egg (peeled)
  • 1 glass bottle (with an opening slightly smaller than the egg)
  • Matches or a small piece of paper
  • Vegetable oil (optional, to help the egg slide)

Procedure

  1. Make sure the egg is slightly larger than the bottle opening.
  2. Light a small piece of paper and quickly drop it into the bottle.
  3. Immediately place the egg on top of the bottle opening.
  4. Watch as the egg gets sucked into the bottle!

Observations

The flame heats the air inside the bottle, causing it to expand. When the flame goes out, the air cools and contracts, creating lower pressure inside. The higher external air pressure then pushes the egg into the bottle.
