Cloud in a Jar Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this fun and simple experiment, you can create a cloud inside a jar! By manipulating air pressure, youâ€™ll be able to see how clouds form in the atmosphere.

Materials Required

  • 1 large glass jar
  • 1 small cup (or a metal can)
  • Hot water
  • Ice cubes
  • Aerosol spray (optional, or you can use matches)

Procedure

  1. Pour hot water into the glass jar (about 1/4 full) and swirl it around to heat the sides of the jar.
  2. Place the small cup or can inside the jar, then fill the top with ice cubes.
  3. After a minute, you can either spray a small amount of aerosol into the jar or light a match and quickly drop it in before covering the jar with the ice.
  4. Watch as a cloud forms inside the jar!

Observations

The warm water creates moisture that rises up inside the jar. When it meets the cold air at the top, the moisture condenses into tiny water droplets, forming a cloud. The aerosol particles or smoke from the match help to condense the water vapor.
