DIY Water Filtration Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this DIY water filtration experiment, you'll build a simple water filter using natural materials. This experiment demonstrates how filtration works and helps us understand how clean drinking water is produced.

Materials Required

  • Plastic bottle or funnel
  • Gravel
  • Sand
  • Activated charcoal (optional, but improves filtration)
  • Coffee filter or cotton
  • Dirty water (muddy water for testing)
  • A container to collect filtered water

Building the Water Filter

Cut the bottom off a plastic bottle and turn it upside down to create a funnel. This will hold the filtration layers and allow water to pass through.

Procedure

  1. Place a coffee filter or a layer of cotton at the bottom of the bottle (small opening).
  2. Add a layer of activated charcoal (if available) to remove impurities and odors.
  3. Layer sand over the charcoal to filter fine particles.
  4. Add a layer of gravel to trap larger debris.
  5. Slowly pour the dirty water through the filter and collect the filtered water in a clean container.

Observations

As the water moves through the layers, it becomes clearer. The gravel removes large particles, the sand captures smaller impurities, and the charcoal (if used) absorbs chemicals and odors. This demonstrates the basic principles of natural water filtration.
