DIY Lava Lamp Experiment

Overview

In this exciting DIY project, youâ€™ll create your own lava lamp using simple household ingredients! This experiment demonstrates how liquids of different densities interact and create mesmerizing bubbles that resemble the movement of lava.

Materials Required

  • 1 clear plastic or glass bottle
  • Water
  • Vegetable oil (or any other light oil)
  • Food coloring
  • 1 Alka-Seltzer tablet (or any fizzy tablet)

Procedure

  1. Fill the bottle about two-thirds full with vegetable oil.
  2. Fill the remaining space with water, leaving a little room at the top of the bottle.
  3. Add a few drops of food coloring to the water and let it mix with the oil.
  4. Break an Alka-Seltzer tablet into smaller pieces and drop one piece into the bottle.
  5. Watch as the food coloring begins to bubble up and move like lava inside the bottle!

Observations

The oil and water do not mix because the oil is less dense than the water. When the Alka-Seltzer reacts with the water, it produces bubbles of gas that push the colored water upward through the oil, creating a lava lamp effect.
