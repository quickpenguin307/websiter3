Oobleck Experiment

DiscereMinds | Empowering STEM Education

Overview

In this experiment, you'll create a strange and fascinating substance called oobleck. Oobleck behaves like a liquid when handled gently but acts like a solid when force is applied. This experiment demonstrates the properties of non-Newtonian fluids.

Materials Required

  • 1 cup of cornstarch
  • ½ cup of water
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Bowl for mixing
  • Spoon (optional, but hands work best!)

Making Oobleck

Oobleck is easy to make and fun to play with. Just follow these simple steps:

Procedure

  1. Pour 1 cup of cornstarch into a mixing bowl.
  2. Slowly add ½ cup of water while stirring.
  3. Add a few drops of food coloring if desired and mix thoroughly.
  4. Continue mixing until the substance reaches a thick, gooey consistency.

Observations

Try pressing down on the oobleck with your fingers—it should feel solid. Then let your fingers sink in slowly, and it will behave like a liquid. This happens because oobleck is a non-Newtonian fluid, meaning its viscosity changes under stress.
